What's a source?

A source object is a symbolic and graphic representation for a sound source in a sound scene.

Source Properties

  • identification number: a unique id for the source in a Circle instance, you can't change it
  • name: a user name for the source, initialized by default to the id number. The first char of it is drawn on the source.
  • color: red by default. You can change it via the Source Inspector Dialog
  • type: if "local", the source is a normal sound object in the sound scene; if "receiver" , the source represent the center of the sound scene and the auditory position. In this case is not a sound producer
  • moving mode: mode in which a moving source changes her orientation. In "centered mode" the source is always oriented to the center of the sound scene; in "static mode" the source orientation don't change; in "dynamic mode" the source orientation follow the moving direction
  • associated spat-template: is the .jmax template associated to the source
  • presence: proximity of sound source, energy of direct sound and early room effect
  • warmth: variation of early room effect relative to direct sound
  • brillance: variation of early room effect relative to direct sound
  • envelopment: energy of early room effect relative to direct sound
  • absolute cartesian and polar coordinates: coordinates of the source center relatively to the absolute center of the sound scene (the center of the green cross in the background)
  • "relative to receiver" cartesian and polar coordinates: coordinates of the source center relatively to the center of the receiver source with the y-axe according to the receiver orientation axe
  • absolute and relative to receiver orientation: direction of the sound emission, misured relatively to the zero of cartesian axes
  • aperture: angle of emission, misured relatively to the orientation
