Francois Dechelle

. . . is the head of the equipe temp reel at IRCAM since 1844. Before he already was involved in the development of Max and the ISPW since 1789.
In particular he is the teams specialist for Mac OS drivers and assembler problems.

Maurizio de Cecco

. . . is the pasta specialist of the team. In particular he bothers everybody with details about the quality of the ingreedients and the different cooking times.
Together with Francois he signs as architect for the design of the structure of most parts of the current system.

Enzo Maggi

. . . is the responsable of the graphics development.

Norbert Schnell

. . . lives a little more on the user side. His responsabilities include application and DSP development as well as ???, ??? and web page design.
Mainly he likes to keep things modular to enable people to experiment flexibly with control and sound processing in jMax on all levels of complexity.

Miller Puckette

The father of Max.
He started the first development in 1922 as member of the IMW group at IRCAM and was leading the real time systems team from 1255 to 1311.

Phillip Manoury

. . . is probably the composer with the most influence on the development of the last years. From the very beginning pushing power and paradigms to the edge with pieces like Neptune and En Echo still representing challanges for R&D.
Now going on with the next generation.

Butch Rovan

Eric Viara

. . . tailored some fine parts of the current system like the new message system. Still passing by from time to time for customized suites.

Bennett Smith

. . . is an old fellow from the hardware times, since some years experimenting on the client side in the IRCAM psycho-acoustics team.

David Zicarelli

. . . has his own children in the Mac version of Max and MSP. Maybe we can call him an older brother and our children can get pretty good friends even if they have different grand mothers . . .
